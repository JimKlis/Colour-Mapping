Mapping of Colour onto Musical Notes

The Laws of Harmonious Colouring

David Ramsay Hay - 1838

David Ramsay Hay, a 19th-century Scottish artist and theorist, developed a system that mapped colours onto musical notes in an attempt to express a universal harmony underlying both sound and vision. In his work The Laws of Harmonious Colouring, he proposed that colour and music are governed by analogous principles of proportion and harmony.
